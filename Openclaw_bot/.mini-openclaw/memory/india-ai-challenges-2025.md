Main Challenges for India to Catch Up in AI (2025):

1. SKILL SHORTAGE - Significant talent gap in AI development; government launching IndiaAI FutureSkills Initiative with Data Labs in tier 2/3 cities
2. DATA PRIVACY & ETHICS - Privacy concerns and ethical considerations need addressing (Carnegie reference, Wikipedia)
3. COMPUTE INFRASTRUCTURE - Lacks world-class compute; plans announced Jan 2025 for own LLM to rival ChatGPT/DeepSeek (Indian Express, Jan 31, 2025)
4. LIMITED INVESTMENT - Funding lags global leaders; private sector focuses on business automation vs foundational research
5. CYBERSECURITY - Increased AI-powered cyberattacks targeting organizations
6. BRAIN DRAIN - Skilled professionals migrate to countries with better opportunities
7. LANGUAGE BARRIERS - Need for multi-lingual AI (initiatives: KissanAI's Dhenu model, C-DAC's GIST project)
8. DATA QUALITY - Need for large-scale quality datasets; IndiaAI Data Labs training curators/annotators

Government Response: IndiaAI Mission (March 6, 2025) with Application Development Initiative, Innovation Challenge, FutureSkills Initiative

Sources: Wikipedia "Artificial intelligence in India", Indian Express (Barik 2025), Analytics India Magazine
